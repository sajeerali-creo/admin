Strategic Checkpoints

Repetition

the act of practicing the same shots or movements over and over again to improve muscle memory and consistency.

Recognizing Strength

involves identifying and utilizing the skills and abilities that you excel at, such as a powerful serve or strong forehand.

Game Style

refers to the way a player prefers to play, whether it be aggressive and offensive, or defensive and strategic.

  • Aggressive Baseliner
  • Counter Puncher
  • Serve and Vollyer
  • All Court Player

Surfaces

refer to the different types of courts that matches can be played on, such as grass, clay, hard court, or carpet.

  • Hard
  • Clay
  • Grass
  • Carpet
  • Indoor

Game Situations

are specific scenarios that occur during a match, such as serving for the set or facing a break point.

Match Situations

flow and momentum of a match, including factors such as the score, level of play, and mental aspects.

Tournament Situations

involve the pressure and intensity of competing in a multi-round event against a variety of opponents.
