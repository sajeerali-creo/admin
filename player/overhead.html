Preparation
Get into ready position
Use continental grip
Non-dominant hand on throat for stability.
Stance
Start with your feet shoulder-width apart
Turn your body slightly towards the net
Bend your knees and get into a balanced stance
Keep your non-hitting hand on the racket for balance
Stay on the balls of your feet for quick movement
Step forward with your front foot as you swing the racket
Backswing
Start with a semi-open stance for a one-handed backhand or a closed stance for a two-handed backhand
Hold the racket with a firm grip and bring it back with a controlled motion
Keep your non-hitting hand on the racket for support and balance
Turn your shoulders and hips as you bring the racket back
Keep the racket head up and parallel to the ground at the top of your backswing
Focus on keeping your wrist firm and your elbow close to your body
Maintain a smooth and consistent backswing tempo
Keep your eyes on the ball at all times
Contact point
Eyes on the ball
Contact point above the head
Arm extended fully towards the ball
Snap your wrist
Extend your arms
Follow through
Racket head pointing towards the target
Finish with your racket across your body
